Mies van der Rohe 7 Eleven

Blender3D

If I had my time again - or I had a spare five years on my hands, I'd become an architect (or an Industrial designer or...) Instead, I have a month. What to do? Pick a minimalist architectural classic to learn Blender3D. Mies van der Rohe's Farnsworth House is as minimal as it gets. Two slabs and some glass - how hard can it be?

While it doesn't look like much, this is the first time i've felt joy using a computer in years (javascript fatigue anyone?). I'd forgotten what it was like to design something i'm actually interested in. Hours flew by. I lost track of time. It was like meditating.

And then I got to windows.

A (slight) detour

After trying to render something remotely resembling glass, I discovered the great Blender Guru tutorials. Of course, being a architecture tradegic I signed up for Architecture Academy as well. And down the rabbit hole I went.

The Blender Guru tutorials where great but intense - and extreme overkill to learn how to render the Farnsworth House. It didn't take long to realise there's a big difference between realism and photo realism. Once you make one element of an image photorealistic, every aspect of the image has to be photorealistic - otherwise you get into the architectural uncanny valley.
